Deep Q-Networks

I built a DQN which teaches itself to play Space Invaders.

Overview

Deep Q-Networks (DQNs) combine deep learning with reinforcement learning to create general purpose agents that can learn to do tasks without needing pre-inputted data. They learn through trial and error.

I used a DQN to learn how to play Space Invaders but the same network could right now learn to play any other Atari game. With deep reinforcement learning (DRL), we can basically train an agent to manuever any sort of scenario that has a finite number of possible actions.

We can also use DRL to solve problems that have been near impossible for humans. Reinforcement learning allows machines to come up with new approaches to problems and aren't capped by any data or preprogrammed rules we give them. Right now, DRL is being tested in self driving cars, robotics and industrial automation, stock prediction and finance and figuring out how to solve the protein folding problem.
